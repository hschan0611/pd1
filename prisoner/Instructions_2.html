This is a computerized decision-making experiment in which you will be paid based on the decisions you make. The entire experiment will take approximately 90 minutes. At the end of the experiment, you will be paid privately and in cash for your participation. All information collected will remain anonymous, ensuring that neither the experimenter nor other participants can link your identity to your decisions. We are interested in your individual choices, so there are no right or wrong answers. You will also receive a printed copy of these instructions, which you may refer to at any time during the session.

The Experiment

In this experiment, you will participate in two games. Your decisions in the first game will not affect what happens in the second game. You will be able to earn points in both games, and your final payment will depend on the points you earn in both games.

Each game consists of sequences, referred to as matches. The length of a match, referred to as the number of rounds, is randomly determined. After each round, there is a {{ continuation_chance }}% probability that the match will continue for at least another round. So, for instance:

  • If you are in round 2, the probability there will be a third round is {{ continuation_chance }}%.
  • If you are in round 9, the probability there will be a tenth round is also {{ continuation_chance }}%.

This is how the length of a match is determined: at the end of each round, the computer rolls a fair 100-sided die.

  • If the die comes up {{ die_threshold_plus_one }} or more, the match ends.
  • If the die comes up {{ continuation_chance }} or less, the match continues to another round.

Once a match ends, you will be randomly paired with another person for a new match.

In each round, you and the person you are paired with choose simultaneously between two actions, Action 1 and Action 2.

Once you and the person you are paired with have made your choices, those choices will be shown.

After completing this first game, you will participate in a second game with different payoffs. The format and rules will remain the same, but the specific payoff amounts will change.

Payment

In this experiment, you will earn points through the decisions that you make. Your earnings will be determined as follows:

  • Game 1: At the end of the session, one of the matches from Game 1 will be chosen at random. You are paid the sum of all points you earned from your decisions in that selected match.
  • Game 2: Similarly, one of the matches from Game 2 will be chosen at random. You are paid the sum of all points you earned from your decisions in that selected match.

All points are converted at a rate of 32 points = $1, and you also receive a $8 show-up fee.
