In this experiment, you will participate in two parts: Part 1 and Part 2.

The two parts are independent: your decisions in Part 1 will not affect what happens in Part 2. You will earn points in both parts, and your final payment will be based on your combined earnings.

Each part consists of sequences, referred to as matches. The length of a match, referred to as the number of rounds, is randomly determined. After each round, there is a {{ continuation_chance }}% probability that the match will continue for at least another round. For example:

  • If you are in round 2, the probability there will be a third round is {{ continuation_chance }}%.
  • If you are in round 9, the probability there will be a tenth round is also {{ continuation_chance }}%.

This is how the length of a match is determined: at the end of each round, the computer rolls a fair 100-sided die.

  • If the die comes up {{ die_threshold_plus_one }} or more, the match ends.
  • If the die comes up {{ continuation_chance }} or less, the match continues to another round.

Once a match ends, you will be randomly paired with another person for a new match.

In each round, you and the person you are paired with choose simultaneously between two actions, Action 1 and Action 2.

This table shows the payoffs associated with each choice in Part 1:

The first entry in each cell represents your payoff, while the second entry represents the payoff of the person you are matched with.

  • If you select Action 1 and the other selects Action 1, you each make {{ Constants.both_cooperate_payoff_1 }} points.
  • If you select Action 1 and the other selects Action 2, you make {{ Constants.betrayed_payoff_1 }} points while the other makes {{ Constants.betray_payoff_1 }} points.
  • If you select Action 2 and the other selects Action 1, you make {{ Constants.betray_payoff_1 }} points while the other makes {{ Constants.betrayed_payoff_1 }} points.
  • If you select Action 2 and the other selects Action 2, you each make {{ Constants.both_defect_payoff_1 }} points.

Once you and the person you are paired with have made your choices, those choices will be shown.

After completing Part 1, you will participate in Part 2. The format and rules will remain the same, but the specific payoff amounts may change.

In this experiment, you will earn points through the decisions that you make. Your earnings will be determined as follows:

  • Part 1: At the end of the session, one of the matches from Part 1 will be chosen at random. You are paid the sum of all points you earned from your decisions in that selected match.
  • Part 2: Similarly, one of the matches from Part 2 will be chosen at random. You are paid the sum of all points you earned from your decisions in that selected match.

All points are converted at a rate of 25 points = $1, and you also receive a $5 show-up fee.
