This experiment has four parts; the following instructions are for Part 1.

In this experiment you will be repeatedly matched with a randomly selected person in the room. During each match, you will be asked to make decisions over a sequence of rounds.

In each round, you and the person you are paired with choose simultaneously between two actions, Action 1 and Action 2. The points you can obtain in each round of a match depend on your choice and the choice of the person you are paired with.

This table shows the payoffs associated with each choice in Part 1:

The Other Participant
Action 1 Action 2
You Action 1 {{ Constants.both_cooperate_payoff_1 }}, {{ Constants.both_cooperate_payoff_1 }} {{ Constants.betrayed_payoff_1 }}, {{ Constants.betray_payoff_1 }}
Action 2 {{ Constants.betray_payoff_1 }}, {{ Constants.betrayed_payoff_1 }} {{ Constants.both_defect_payoff_1 }}, {{ Constants.both_defect_payoff_1 }}

The first entry in each cell represents your payoff, while the second entry represents the payoff of the person you are matched with.

  • If you select Action 1 and the other selects Action 1, you each make {{ Constants.both_cooperate_payoff_1 }} points.
  • If you select Action 1 and the other selects Action 2, you make {{ Constants.betrayed_payoff_1 }} points while the other makes {{ Constants.betray_payoff_1 }} points.
  • If you select Action 2 and the other selects Action 1, you make {{ Constants.betray_payoff_1 }} points while the other makes {{ Constants.betrayed_payoff_1 }} points.
  • If you select Action 2 and the other selects Action 2, you each make {{ Constants.both_defect_payoff_1 }} points.

At the end of each round, you will see your choice (Action 1 or Action 2), the choice of the person you were paired with (Action 1 or Action 2), and the points you earned.

Part 1 consists of matches. The length of a match, referred to as the number of rounds, is randomly determined. After each round, there is a {{ continuation_chance }}% probability that the match will continue for at least another round. For example:

  • If you are in round 2, the probability there will be a third round is {{ continuation_chance }}%.
  • If you are in round 9, the probability there will be a tenth round is also {{ continuation_chance }}%.

This is how the length of a match is determined: at the end of each round, the computer rolls a fair 100-sided die.

  • If the die comes up {{ die_threshold_plus_one }} or more, the match ends.
  • If the die comes up {{ continuation_chance }} or less, the match continues to another round.

Once a match ends, you will be randomly paired with another person for a new match.

In this experiment, you will earn points through the decisions that you make. At the end of the session, one of the matches from Part 1 will be chosen at random. You are paid the sum of all points you earned from your decisions in that selected match.

All points are converted to dollars at a rate of 25 points = $1, and you also receive a $5 show-up fee.
