Reminder of Instructions for Belief Task

To indicate your belief, you will use the slider shown on the screen. The position of the slider represents your best assessment of the likelihood (expressed as a percentage) that the person you are paired with chooses Action 1 or Action 2.

Examples
  • 60%: You believe that the person you are paired with has a 60% chance of selecting Action 1 (and a 40% chance of selecting Action 2).
  • 30%: You believe that the person you are paired with has a 30% chance of selecting Action 1 (and a 70% chance of selecting Action 2).
Payment

In Part 1, one match will be randomly selected to count toward your payment, and you will receive the points associated with your choices. Furthermore, a different match will be randomly selected, and the computer will randomly choose one round from that match for payment in the belief task. The belief that you report in that round will determine your chance of winning a prize of 50 points.

To determine your payment in the belief task, the computer will randomly draw two numbers between 0 and 100. The two draws are independent, meaning that the outcome of the first draw in no way affects the outcome of the second draw.

  • If the person you are paired with chose Action 1 in that round, and the number you indicated as the likelihood that the other chose 1 is larger than either of the two draws, you will win the prize.
  • If the person you are paired with chose Action 2 in that round, and the number you indicated as the likelihood that the other chose 2 is larger than either of the two draws, you will win the prize.
